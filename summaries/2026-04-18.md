## Section 1: World News — Top Stories

### 1. Iran Closes Strait of Hormuz Again as US Naval Blockade Continues

Iran has again closed the Strait of Hormuz to commercial shipping, blaming an ongoing US naval blockade that has effectively halted all seaborne trade in and out of Iranian ports. The confrontation follows weeks of escalating tension: the US Navy implemented the blockade on 13 April after peace talks in Islamabad broke down, with Admiral Brad Cooper of US Central Command announcing that 23 ships had been turned back since it began. On Friday, Iranian and US officials briefly signalled an opening — with Iran declaring the strait open and President Trump announcing "very good conversations" with Tehran — but by Saturday Iran reversed course, citing Washington's refusal to lift the blockade. Indian-flagged oil tankers came under fire from Iranian gunboats in the waterway. The strait carries roughly 20 million barrels of oil per day, around 20 per cent of global seaborne oil trade, and Brent crude prices have risen sharply.

### 3. Sudan's Civil War Enters a Fourth Year of Catastrophe

This week marked three years since Sudan's devastating civil war erupted between the Sudanese Armed Forces and the Rapid Support Forces (RSF), with the United Nations warning the world is failing to respond adequately to one of the worst humanitarian crises on the planet. Nearly 14 million people have been displaced and estimates suggest as many as 400,000 people have died. The first quarter of 2026 alone saw nearly 700 civilians killed in RSF drone strikes, including a deadly attack on a hospital in White Nile state that decimated its medical staff. Famine conditions persist across Darfur and Kordofan, and neither side has achieved a decisive military advantage as the war grinds into its fourth year.

### 4. Italy Suspends Defence Agreement with Israel

Italy has halted the automatic renewal of a long-standing defence cooperation agreement with Israel, marking a significant diplomatic shift from one of Jerusalem's closest European allies. Prime Minister Giorgia Meloni announced the suspension at an event in Verona, citing "the current situation" — widely understood to refer to Israeli strikes on Lebanon and allegations that Israeli forces fired warning shots at Italian UN peacekeepers in southern Lebanon. The agreement, which covered joint military research, training and defence industry cooperation, was due to auto-renew for a further five years on 13 April. Israel's Foreign Minister Gideon Saar downplayed the move, saying the pact had "never materialised" in any substantial way.

### 1. Iran Conflict Pushes Up Fuel Prices and Threatens UK Economy

The crisis in the Middle East is already feeding through to higher energy costs in Britain, with Prime Minister Keir Starmer warning that rising fuel prices will have wider economic consequences and that the UK is pushing for diplomatic solutions to reopen key trade routes. The IMF has downgraded its UK growth forecast to just 0.8 per cent for 2026, and economists now expect inflation to climb towards 3.5 per cent in the second half of the year — well above the Bank of England's 2 per cent target. Britain's vulnerability as a net energy importer means the Strait of Hormuz crisis poses a particular risk to households and businesses already squeezed by high costs.
